    Case Study: Website Redesign

    Redesign the user flow sequence of the Lostandfound.com website to be more streamlined for an improved experience. See the Before and After below to see what their website looked like at the time of my redesign. Since then, I noticed they've made some improvements. Hmmm. Maybe they saw my work.

    Tools

    • UXPin using Bootstrap Elements

    Goal

    Eliminate duplicate information, simplify a cluttered interface, combine tabs for efficiency, and make the human element more prominent by displaying success stories through social media and by appealing to the user's emotional response. A user who finds an item and wishes to report it on the website will be able to report the claim quickly and easily then get a payoff after finishing.

    Features Added: After completing a found item report, the user will receive a thank you from Lostandfound.com , a discount off goods or services from one of the website advertisers, and Pay It Forward points redeemable for gift cards once a certain amount is reached. When the lost item successfully gets matched to its property owner, a message will be shared to the user's social media connections (with permission) bragging on their behalf about their act of kindness and about how the user helped someone retrieve their property.

    Case Study: App Design for Reserving Services at a Hotel

    Design a user experience for a mobile app that allows you to book appointments for services at a boutique hotel.

    Process

    • User Research
    • Mind Mapping
    • User Personas
    • Low-Fidelity Sketches
    • Paper Prototyping
    • User Testing

    Tools

    • PopApp
    • Video

    Goal

    Guests can book a massage appointment at the hotel spa by using the iPad provided in their room. By navigating through the app sequence the user can browse a menu of services offered, book or change an appointment, and create a new account.
